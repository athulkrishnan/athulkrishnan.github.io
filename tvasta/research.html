R&D at Tvasta

3D Printing is a new frontier, ripe for growth and requiring pioneers to guide its path. We are committed to growth of this wild west, and industry-academia led research is at the heart of what we do.

The Research Group is a multi-disciplined team pursuing research in several areas to make a complete technological solution viable. Research is being attempted in the area of 3D Printing Process, Material Development, Algorithms for Path Planning and Construction Automation.

  1. 3D Printing Process The Research Group has built a Customized Concrete 3D Printer for testing 3D Printing process. The machine was designed and built in house. The machine is currently undergoing testing with respect to various aspects of 3D Printing.
  2. Material Development The Research Group is currently pursuing development of customized Concrete Mixes for 3D Printing process. The Concrete mixes developed are aimed to address the several changes that the construction process undergoes when 3D Printing is applied. The strengths of the 3D Printing process need to be leveraged and the shortcomings of the process addressed with this customized mix design.
  3. Algorithms for Path Planning Software solutions are currently being developed to control the 3D Printer and the Auxiliary equipment during the 3D Printing process from a centralized facility. The aim is to adopt the existing 3D Modeling and Building Solutions in the construction industry and leverage and adapt them for the purpose of 3D Printing.
  4. Construction Automation To develop an end to end 3D Printing process that is suitable for printing using Concrete, one needs to develop several auxiliary solutions that enable the process. These auxiliary equipments need to be integrated with the 3D Printer to enable complete automation of the process. In order to achieve this, several existing equipments have been integrated with the 3D Printer. In several areas where off-the-shelf solutions were not available, equipments were designed and manufactured in-house.
